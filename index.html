About Me

I am a recent graduate of UC Santa Barbara devoted to combating climate change through data driven analysis. I am currently developing machine learning methods for spatial network data and for integrating time-varying structures of covariates in high-dimensional regression problems. I am also passionate about developing accessible software for practioners to use in real-time decision making. As I progress in my academic career, I aim to apply my knowledge of statistics and machine learning to solve environmental problems and help create a sustainable future.

My research interests include:

  • Spatial statistics and network problems
  • High-dimensional and nonparametric statistics
  • Estimating graphical models
